Politics

Austria is a federal parliamentary republic with a president as the head of state and a chancellor as the head of government. The country follows a multi-party system, with the Austrian Parliament consisting of two chambers: the National Council and the Federal Council.

National Meals

Austria is known for its delicious cuisine. Some popular national meals include Wiener Schnitzel (breaded and fried veal or pork cutlet), Sachertorte (a famous Viennese chocolate cake), Tafelspitz (boiled beef with horseradish sauce), and Apfelstrudel (apple strudel).

States in Austria

Austria consists of nine federal states, also known as Bundesländer. They are Burgenland, Carinthia, Lower Austria, Upper Austria, Salzburg, Styria, Tyrol, Vorarlberg, and Vienna. Each state has its own distinct characteristics, cultural traditions, and natural landscapes.

Location of Austria

Austria is a landlocked country located in Central Europe. It shares borders with eight countries: Germany, Czech Republic, Slovakia, Hungary, Slovenia, Italy, Switzerland, and Liechtenstein. The country's central location makes it easily accessible and offers diverse landscapes ranging from the stunning Austrian Alps to charming lakes and historic cities.
